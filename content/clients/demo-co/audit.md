# AI Workflow Audit Report

**Client:** Demo Co
**Date:** March 1, 2026
**Prepared by:** AI Workflow Accelerator

---

## Executive Summary

Demo Co's engineering team currently spends approximately **18 hours per week** on manual processes that are suitable for AI automation. This audit identified **4 automation opportunities** focused on developer productivity and incident management.

## Current State Assessment

| Process | Weekly Hours | Pain Level | Automation Potential |
|---------|-------------|------------|---------------------|
| Incident triage | 8 hrs | High | Excellent |
| Release notes drafting | 4 hrs | Medium | Good |
| Code review summaries | 3 hrs | Low | Good |
| On-call handoff reports | 3 hrs | Medium | Moderate |

## Priority Recommendations

### 1. Incident Triage Automation (Priority: Critical)

**Current process:** On-call engineer manually reviews alerts, correlates logs, and determines severity before escalating.

**Proposed automation:** AI-powered alert classification that correlates monitoring signals, assigns severity, and routes to the appropriate team.

**Expected impact:** 8 hours/week saved, faster incident response times.

### 2. Release Notes Generator (Priority: High)

**Current process:** Engineering lead manually reviews merged PRs and writes release notes each sprint.

**Proposed automation:** Claude API analyzes merged PRs, generates categorized release notes, and posts draft to Slack for review.

**Expected impact:** 4 hours/week saved, consistent formatting across releases.

## ROI Projection

- **Monthly time saved:** ~48 hours (12 hrs/week x 4 weeks)
- **Equivalent cost savings:** $3,600-4,800/month
- **Audit investment:** $1,500
- **Payback period:** < 2 weeks
